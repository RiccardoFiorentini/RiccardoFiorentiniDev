Unleashing the power of GPUs in genomics analyses: The PairHMM Use Case
Developed a GPU-based implementation of the Pair-HMM forward algorithm boosting flexibility by removing input limits and achieving a remarkable speedup of over 5 times compared to existing methods. Recognized for excellence, the project was accepted for the "Best Student Research Video & Poster Competition" at the EUROCON2023 conference.
Abstract
In the personalized medicine domain, genome analysis is crucial to highlight genetic variants and provide customized treatments to each patient. HaplotypeCaller, developed by the Broad Institute, represents the de-facto standard tool for variant identification and relies on the Pair-HMM algorithm. However, HaplotypeCaller's analyses require long execution times due to a bottleneck represented by the Pair-HMM. Moreover, given this algorithmâ€™s compute-intensiveness, general-purpose CPUs exacerbate this limitation. Thus, offloading it to hardware accelerators is a suitable solution to speed up the execution. However, the performance achieved by State-ofthe-Art works comes with constraints on flexibility, jeopardizing their usability. This project aims at accelerating the PairHMM algorithm on GPU, incorporating various optimizations to address the computational challenges and overcome the issues of the currently-available solutions.
